Landmarks & geography

1. How tall is the CN Tower?

Show answer
553.3 metres, roughly 1,815 feet.

2. In what year did the CN Tower open to the public?

Show answer
1976.

3. What was Toronto's original name before 1834?

Show answer
York.

4. Which Great Lake does Toronto sit on?

Show answer
Lake Ontario.

5. What is the name of Toronto's downtown baseball and football stadium, formerly SkyDome?

Show answer
Rogers Centre.

6. What underground network connects buildings across Toronto's Financial District?

Show answer
The PATH, one of the largest underground pedestrian networks in the world.

Sports

7. In what year did the Toronto Raptors win their first NBA championship?

Show answer
2019.

8. What NHL team plays out of Scotiabank Arena?

Show answer
The Toronto Maple Leafs.

9. In what year did the Toronto Maple Leafs last win the Stanley Cup?

Show answer
1967.

10. What is the Toronto Blue Jays' home stadium called?

Show answer
Rogers Centre, which they share with the CFL's Toronto Argonauts for some events.

11. The Blue Jays won back-to-back World Series titles in which two years?

Show answer
1992 and 1993.

Culture & history

12. Which Toronto-born rapper released the album 'Views'?

Show answer
Drake.

13. What is Toronto's tallest-building nickname for its financial district, home to Canada's major banks?

Show answer
Bay Street.

14. Toronto hosts one of the world's largest street festivals celebrating Caribbean culture. What is it called?

Show answer
Caribana (officially the Toronto Caribbean Carnival).

15. What is Toronto's approximate population, making it Canada's largest city?

Show answer
Roughly 2.9 million people (2025 estimate), for the city proper.

16. Which university, founded in 1827, is Toronto's oldest and largest?

Show answer
The University of Toronto.

17. What is the name of Toronto's main airport, named after a former prime minister?

Show answer
Toronto Pearson International Airport, named after Lester B. Pearson.

18. Toronto is the capital of which Canadian province?

Show answer
Ontario.
