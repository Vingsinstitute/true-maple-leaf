Canada Day history & Confederation

1. On what date is Canada Day celebrated each year?

Show answer
July 1 — the anniversary of Confederation in 1867.

2. What 1867 law united the colonies into the Dominion of Canada?

Show answer
The British North America Act.

3. What was Canada Day called before 1982?

Show answer
Dominion Day.

4. How many provinces originally joined Confederation in 1867?

Show answer
Four — Ontario, Quebec, Nova Scotia and New Brunswick.

5. Who became Canada's first Prime Minister in 1867?

Show answer
Sir John A. Macdonald.

6. Which province joined in 1871 after being promised a railway?

Show answer
British Columbia.

Canadian symbols & Canadiana

7. What are the two official colours of the Canadian flag?

Show answer
Red and white.

8. How many points are on the maple leaf on the flag?

Show answer
Eleven.

9. What is Canada's national winter sport?

Show answer
Ice hockey. (The national summer sport is lacrosse.)

10. What does the motto 'A Mari usque ad Mare' mean?

Show answer
'From Sea to Sea' (Latin).

11. Which one-dollar coin is nicknamed the 'loonie'?

Show answer
The one-dollar coin (the two-dollar coin is the 'toonie').

12. What national police force is known by the initials RCMP?

Show answer
The Royal Canadian Mounted Police.

13. Which line from 'O Canada' celebrates the country's north?

Show answer
"The True North strong and free."

14. In what year did the current maple leaf flag become official?

Show answer
1965.

Geography & the modern country

15. What is the capital city of Canada?

Show answer
Ottawa, Ontario.

16. Ottawa sits on the border of Ontario and which province?

Show answer
Quebec.

17. Which territory, created in 1999, is Canada's newest?

Show answer
Nunavut.

18. What are Canada's two official federal languages?

Show answer
English and French.

19. New Canadians are often welcomed on Canada Day at what kind of ceremony?

Show answer
Citizenship ceremonies.

20. Canada Day's biggest official party is held on Parliament Hill in which city?

Show answer
Ottawa.
